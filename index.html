Welcome to the OpenHeart Project.

Medical devices are expensive. It seems like this is the universally accepted truth among healthcare professionals. But why is this the case? The open source community has the power to create cheap, medically useful devices that make a difference in the world.

The OpenHeart Project's goal to develop an open source FDA compliant continuous 12-lead heart monitor. We want to create a device that is cheap enough to deploy in underserved parts of the world.

The OpenHeart Project was founded by Mark Newcomb (@mdnewcombjr)
